Project Overview

Background: I decided to take the initiative to challenge myself and step into a new environment. This led me to asking AI, well, ChatGPT in particular to give me a request so I can practice freelancing. And so, they gave me the brief that is listed below.

Brief: "Hi! I'm starting a fictional coffee shop called Bean Scene. Can you create a simple, modern logo with a coffee cup icon and the shop name?"

So, here's my journey below:

Design Journey

Design Process

Concept 1

I decided to play around with Figma for the first time, furthering challenging myself. My initial approach was to engrave the company's name into the mug for a modern sleek look, while coffee beans were utilised to represent steam. Although I still stand with this concept, the company's name became invisible

Concept 2

Still keeping the mug, I decided to have part of the company's name acting as the steam. This outcome seemed to be more playful, not looking modern anymore

Concept 3

I decided to move to Canva to go for a more simplistic approach, but it felt unoriginal. Here are 2 versions of the same design, reminding me that different colours suit different things

Final Design

Combining all of my concepts and other ideas I had, this was my final design. I wanted to ensure that coffee beans are displayed as it is part of the company's name, but having just coffee beans without the text would have been misreading. I decided to add the curved text around the cup so it resembles a plate or mouth. The red tone background is to create warmth but to keep it modern, perfectly capturing the essence of a welcoming coffee experience

    Project Reflection

    This self-initiated project gave me the drive to start projects without an actual client. I was always convinced that a client had to be a real person, and while I'd always wanted to try freelancing, I was hesitant to begin. Using resources like AI to kickstart the process and overcome my fear turned out to be a great initiative. You don't need a client or a lecturer to provide you with a project just to gain experience, rather use the resources you have. Through this journey, I also started using Figma, giving me an even more competitive edge in the market. I would say integrating the mug into the design was particularly challenging, but I still had fun with it by seeing the various ways the mug could be utilised in the logo. Anyways, remember, the only way to push past your comfort zone and continue growing is by challenging yourself.
